Simulation Result

(1)Uniform Loading of the top cylinder

Under a load of 2N on the top cylinder, the tip of the strand side of the plier was displaced by 21.35mm, and a reaction force of 2.135N was applied to the side of the virus catcher side (Fig 7). At the pinned point, a reaction force of 4.135 N was applied. On the other hand, in the rigid body model, the reaction force at the edge of the virus can be calculated as 2.4286 N (Fig 8). Therefore, the deformation reduces the reaction force by 12% of the calculated value of the rigid body model(Fig 11). Based on the analysis at the millimeter scale above, we could say that, at the nanoscale, a force of about 2nN can be applied to the end of the virus when the plier structure is subjected to a uniform load that totals 2nN. The fact that the force acting on the strands is nearly amplified is probably because about 40% of the strands are located closer to the fixed axis than the end of the catcher side which is distanced by 82.5nm from the pinned point. From this point, the case of shifting the load to the tip was also simulated.


(2) Loading on the tip

When a load was applied on the tip of the strands side, the displacement of the tip was 39.5 mm and the reaction force on the side of the virus catcher was 4.108 N(Fig 9). On the other hand, the force in the rigid body model is calculated as 4.786N(Fig10). Thus, the force applied on the strand side was amplified more than double on the end of virus catcher side, and the deformation reduced the reaction force by about 14% of the rigid body model (Fig11).

Simulation Consideration

From the simulations, leverage could work as a mechanism to amplitude the force from hybridization as long as it is around a few nN, although there would be an effect of deformation. In the case of loading 2nN on the tip, the difference in reaction force from the rigid model was around 10%. In the design section, we calculated the total force by hybridization as 14nN. To prevent a large deformation, the force should be reduced by adopting fewer numbers of strands. As for the simulation itself, the relatively large displacements may be beyond the range that can be handled by the linear theory of FEA. For accurate mechanical simulation, simulation with large displacements solution would be required. Finally, the binding points of the pliers, which are constrained by the intermolecular force of the DNA Plier, may not be able to withstand the reaction force, which is the sum of the force applied on the strand side and catcher side and may be separated.
