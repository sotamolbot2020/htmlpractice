Idea

We attempted to create a structure that would crush the membranes of the influenza virus using DNA origami and came up with the nano-plier. We chose this form because previous research on plier-type structures had been published[1](Kuzuya,2011), the idea was familiar to us and easy to understand, and our team believed we could apply the principle of leverage. This structure is composed of four parts: the body of nano-plier, strand, aptamer, and photoresponsive switch. The body of the plier maintains the overall structure and is responsible for transmitting the force generated by the strand to the virus. The strand generates force through the bonding of mutual base pairs by hybridization. The aptamer is a molecule that binds specifically to the influenza virus, binding the virus until the pliers finish closing. The photoresponsive switch deforms its structure when exposed to the visible spectrum, and works as a trigger to close the nano-plier.
