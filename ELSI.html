ELSI

(1)What is ELSI?

ELSI is an abbreviation for ethical, legal, and social implications. This refers to all non-technical challenges that may arise in the research and social implementation of new science and technology.ELSI was suggested in 1988 by James Watson, at the press conference announcing his appointment as director of the Human Genome Project (HGP) The Human Genome Project is a research project that attempts to decipher all human genetic information (human genome).

（２）Ethical issue

The logical problem with our research is that the nano-plier could be used as a weapon. Currently, it targets viruses that harm humans, but in the future, it may be possible to destroy normal cells. The Biological Weapons Convention (BWC) exists to regulate biological weapons. It is a multilateral legal framework signed by 179 countries and territories around the world. However, the targets of this treaty are considered to be bacteriological weapons (biological weapons) and toxin weapons, not nano-pliers composed of DNA. Therefore, it is considered necessary to incorporate nano-pliers and other nanorobots into the BWC.

（３）Legal issue

As for legal issues, there is a problem in the future when it is used on the human body. As the drug is used in the human body, its safety as well as its effectiveness must be guaranteed. This can be achieved by using the clinical process of pharmaceutical products currently in use. The clinical process of pharmaceutical products is regulated by the Pharmaceutical Affairs Law and the Ministerial Ordinance on Standards for Conducting Clinical Trials of Drugs. We believe that they can be applied to nanorobots as well.

（４）social issue

Social issues include the side effects of administration and the fear of putting the nano-plier and other nanorobots in the body. As for the side effects, it is possible to collect as much data as possible through clinical trials to improve the safety of the nanorobots. We believe that the fear of using nanorobots should be eliminated in stages. Various types of nanorobots can deliver existing drugs to targeted locations in the body. Fear of nano-pliers should be reduced by introducing the ones that are easily accepted by the public.
