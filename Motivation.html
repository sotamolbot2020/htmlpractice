Motivation

DNA origami is a technique which involves the folding of long, ring single-stranded DNA (scaffold chain) into designed structures with the aid of short-staple strands. Structure is created by folding the staple strands together with the complementary sequences of the scaffold chains. Researchers have been making various nano-structures with DNA origami such as 2D smiley faces, nano springs, optical tweezers, and so on. However, many movements, which exist under micro-scale, are still not replicated under the nano-scale. We wanted to propose the novel movement: crushing. If we could implement this movement in nano robots, it would be able to crush to inactivate in body viruses by crushing their membranes.
