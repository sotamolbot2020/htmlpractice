Future

We designed a nano-plier that consists of the body, strands with photoresponsive switch, and aptamer binding to the virus. To test the validity of the force amplification mechanism, leverage, we performed finite element analysis on the body structure. Each component of our design, the body structure, strands with a photoresponsive switch, and virus catcher needs to be investigated before they are integrated into the nano-plier.

The most significant problem in realizing the body is its large size (300nm in length), which makes it difficult to fabricate a nano-plier from a single strand of DNA. One of the solutions is the non-hybridization shape recognition, a structure of DNA origami in the work of Thomas Gerling (2015) et al. This may allow the plier's structure to be produced in multiple parts that can be fabricated on a single strand. In addition, the nano structure needs to be researched from the non-mechanical perspective such as molecular interaction and thermal stability. As for the switching parts, experimental studies will be required to investigate whether the strands in the opening state can start and continue to hybridize beyond the distance between the other strands on the other handle until it reaches the closing state. Moreover, the hybridization force of a single strand with the photoresponsive switch needs to be measured.

With regards to virus catcher aptamer, the time-scale of attaching and detaching with virus spikes has to be investigated. If the attachments and detachments occur at a short time scale, compared to that in the dynamics of opening and closing state transition, it is difficult to hold the virus until the virus membrane is broken.

Two further prospects for nano-plier can be assumed. The first is to improve the mechanical properties of plier by integrating DNA origami structures with nanomaterials such as carbon nanotubes and graphene. For example, a material that is less deformable than DNA origami could be used to support the body of the plier. The other is the interlocking action of the virus catcher and the plier. The nano-plier we designed has independent opening and closing of the virus catcher and plier. If we could link the nano-plier to the virus catcher, we could prevent the plier that does not catch the virus from closing.
