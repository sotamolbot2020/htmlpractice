DNA Nano-Plier

　

Absutract

DNA origami, one of the most trendy DNA nano-technologies today, folds DNA into designed structures with aimed function. It was confirmed that DNA origami is able to create simple movements. However, some intricate functions have not been achieved yet. In our study, we focused on the novel movement: crushing. Our crushing target here is the membrane of influenza viruses, which is one of the most common viruses in the world.

To achieve the crushing movement at the nano-scale, we designed a structure which behaves as a plier (nano-plier). The nano-plier can identify influenza virus specifically with an aptamer, attached to the virus binding site. It can hold viruses until the membranes are broken. Then, the nano-plier starts to close, triggered by isomerization of cis-azobenzene into trans-azobenzene (Photoresponsive switch). When the nano-plier closes, the force is generated by transformation of single-stranded DNAs into double-stranded DNAs (nucleic acid hybridization power) to crush the virus. Once there is sufficient power to close the plier's cutting edge, virus membranes are crushed to be broken. We believe that the nano-plier might be able to break other viruses by adjusting to the viruses’ specificity respectively. In the future, the nano-plier should contribute in the medical field to inactivate and remove viruses from our body.
